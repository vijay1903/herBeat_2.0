About us
Abstract

Coronary Heart disease (CHD) affects 16.5 million adults annually is one of the most prominent causes of death and disability. Lack of access to center based cardiac rehabilitation (CBCR), inadequacy and cost associated with CBCR led to a call for re-engineering of secondary prevention (SP) for all patients. A smart watch at mobile phone-based technology system can offer a home-based secondary prevention of CHD for the patients and can help them self-manage their chronic symptoms through a change of unwanted behaviors. Designing of GUIs which motivate the user for the period required for a behavior to change, can be linchpin for successful behavior change.

Introduction

Prevalence of CHD expected to increase by more than 40% by 2035 and women are presenting with acute myocardial infraction (AMI) at a younger age. In 2015, the estimated cost of caring CHD patients was 182 billion in the U.S.; hospitalization cost for more than half of the costs. Compared with men, women with acute coronary syndromes (ACS), CHD, all undergoing coronary revascularization have up to 30% more re-hospitalization within 30 days up to one year of the event. There is a huge lag in the engaging, personalized, home-based cardiac rehabilitation (HBCR) interventions designed specifically for women with CHD.

Objective

The objective of our research is to design effective GUI for a mobile health application grounded on evidence-based BCTs derived from behavior change theoretical constructs . Our study will also help in designing the decision rules that specify what intervention component should be delivered, and when to maximize the probability of the intended effects. We will subsequently test and optimize the prevention of a micro-randomized trial (MRT).

Methods

We have deployed a home-based secondary prevention system, which includes a smartwatch, a mobile application and a web portal at the front end and a HIPPA compliant private cloud system at the back end. While the machine-learning algorithm inside the smartwatch helps in the detection of activity performed by patients, the mobile application, HerBeat, provide accurate feedback and timely intervention to patients based on the activity detected by the watch. While the sensor data captured by the smartwatch transfers itself to the mobile phone over Bluetooth, the mobile application transfers the same to the Azure HIPPA compliant private cloud via wi-Fi or Cellular network. Precise analytics are generated through precessing of sensor-generated data, and the summary data is preserved in the cloud server. A health coach can access the summarized data through a web portal and a customized dashboard in real time.

Results

The prototype of the system has been tested on a group of 10 patients. The complete system is in the process of initiating a large-scale clinical trial.

Conclusion

Our technology-based alternative to CBCR can support patients with heart disease by helping them to increase control over their health conditions through a change of behavior. One of the primary reasons for the impertinent usage of existing mobile applications is the lack of just-in-time adaptive interventions grounded on sound behavior change theories. To trigger the behavior change, we adopted a holistic approach by incorporating eating behavior and stress management along with users' physical activity goals. We have applied multiple behavior change theories to develop the mobile app's user interactions.
